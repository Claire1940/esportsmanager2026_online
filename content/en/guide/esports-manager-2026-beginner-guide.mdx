<Section icon={Swords} title="Economic Management and Mid-Round Calls">

Economic control is the defining skill that separates average managers from championship winners. Running your economy on autopilot by purchasing standard gear every round will quickly ruin your team's financial standing during a series. Every economic decision—whether to save, semi-buy, force buy, or full buy—must be calculated based on your opponent's bank state.

| Economy Option | Cost Level | Team Morale Impact | Best Scenario to Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Full Eco** | Very Low | Minimal | When bank is completely depleted |
| **Force Buy** | Medium | High (if successful) | To break the opponent's winning momentum |
| **Full Buy** | High | Stable | During standard buy rounds |

Force buys should not be treated as desperate, last-second decisions. When timed correctly, a successful force buy can completely shatter the opposing team's economy, triggering a domino effect that forces them into consecutive save rounds. Furthermore, utilizing timeouts before critical rounds allows you to reorganize the buy plan and realign strategies.

<Callout type="warning" title="Timeout Management">
Do not save your timeouts. If your team drops two consecutive rounds due to poor setups, call an immediate timeout to break the opponent's momentum and adjust your defensive positioning.
</Callout>

</Section>
