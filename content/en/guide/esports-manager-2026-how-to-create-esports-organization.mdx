Every player has a specific confidence rating on each map. Forcing an aggressive entry fragger to play passively on a map they dislike will lead to poor executions and unnecessary round losses.

Running an esports organization is a business. Spending recklessly on player transfers without securing sustainable revenue streams will quickly bankrupt your franchise.

### Smart Transfer Market Strategies

Avoid overpaying for talent. The transfer market is highly dynamic, and patience often yields the best deals.

- **Contract Clauses**: Avoid heavy performance bonuses that can inflate your operating costs unexpectedly.
- **Loans & Academy**: Utilize loans and develop academy players to keep your roster competitive without locking up your budget.
- **Timing**: Wait for players to fall into a slump or approach the end of their contracts to sign them at a massive discount.

| Transfer Strategy | Pros | Cons |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Signing Superstars** | Immediate performance boost | Extremely high salary demands |
| **Academy Development** | Low cost, high profit potential | Requires significant time investment |
| **Loan Signings** | Budget flexibility, low risk | Temporary solution, no resale value |

<Callout type="warning" title="Budget Warning">
Always maintain a financial buffer equal to at least three months of player salaries. Unexpected slumps or meta changes may require quick roster adjustments.
</Callout>
