<Section icon={Target} title="First-Season Setup and Roster Management">

Starting a new career requires careful planning. New managers must choose between creating a custom organization from scratch or taking over one of the 325+ real organizations already present in the database. Creating a new organization provides complete control over the club's identity, branding, and starting roster, whereas taking over an established team attaches immediate competitive expectations and pre-existing contract commitments.

To build a stable club during your first season, follow this structured progression route:

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select Your Starting Path">
    Choose to rebuild a brand-new custom organization or take over an existing professional franchise. Review your starting budget, active sponsorships, and tournament calendar before advancing the in-game calendar.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Assemble a Legal Five-Player Lineup">
    Ensure your active roster contains exactly five eligible players. Following recent official updates, AI-controlled teams will now actively sign players to resolve short-handed rosters, making it critical to secure your starting five early.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Define Player Roles and Specialist Attributes">
    Avoid signing players purely based on their overall rating. A functional squad requires a balanced distribution of roles, including an In-Game Leader (IGL), a dedicated AWPer, and an aggressive Entry Fragger.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Recruit Essential Backroom Staff">
    Prioritize hiring a qualified Coach to accelerate player attribute growth and an Analyst to assist with tactical match preparation. Expand your backroom staff only when your monthly revenue can support their recurring salaries.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Establish Weekly Training Programs">
    Design training routines that target your squad's immediate mechanical weaknesses. Balance high-intensity training schedules with recovery sessions to prevent morale drops and physical fatigue.
  </Step>
</Steps>

A balanced starting roster must distribute specialist responsibilities across the five active slots. Forcing players into unfamiliar roles will negatively impact their in-match decision-making and individual ratings.

| Role | Primary Attributes | Tactical Function |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **IGL** | Skill, Grenades, Clutch | Controls team tempo and late-round setups |
| **AWPer** | AWP, Reaction, Clutch | Serves as the primary sniper for long-range picks |
| **Entry** | Rifle, Reaction, Pistol | Takes aggressive opening duels to secure map space |
| **Support** | Grenades, Rifle, Pistol | Throws utility to enable trades and site executes |

<Section icon={Swords} title="Transfer Market Operations and Contract Negotiation">

The transfer market in Esports Manager 2026 is dynamic and highly responsive to club prestige, player performance, and contract lengths. Managers must navigate various acquisition routes to upgrade their starting lineups without bankrupting the organization.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Free Agents">
    - **No transfer fees required**
    - Ideal for filling immediate roster gaps
    - High-prestige players may reject weak clubs
  </Card>
  <Card title="Paid Transfers">
    - **Requires upfront transfer fees**
    - Directly upgrades key starting positions
    - Scaled closely with current player ratings
  </Card>
  <Card title="Player Loans">
    - **Up to two active incoming loans**
    - Temporary quality for tight budgets
    - Original contracts are preserved
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

When negotiating contracts, pay close attention to the player's demands regarding salary, contract duration, and squad status. Recent updates have introduced realistic wage scaling, meaning a player's market value and salary expectations will fluctuate based on their active playing time.

| Route | Upfront Cost | Key Benefit | Roster Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Free Agent** | None | Low budget | Fills immediate roster gaps |
| **Paid Transfer** | Transfer Fee | High immediate quality | Upgrades starting lineup |
| **Loan In** | Negotiated Fee | Temporary depth | Solves short-term role issues |
| **Loan Out** | None | Saves salary space | Develops young bench prospects |

Managers can also utilize the loan market to send developmental prospects to weaker teams, allowing them to gain valuable competitive experience while keeping their long-term contracts intact. You can recall loaned players early if your main squad suffers an unexpected roster emergency, though this action incurs a reduced administrative fee.

<Callout type="info" title="Bench Wage Reduction Mechanic">
If a contracted player spends more than one month on the bench without active playtime, their wage demands will automatically drop by 30%, reflecting their reduced squad status.
</Callout>
